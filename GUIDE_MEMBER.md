# Team OS — Team Member Guide

You're on a team that uses Team OS to stay organized and healthy. This guide
tells you what you need to do, what protects you, and how to get the most
out of it. It takes 5 minutes to read.

---

## What is Team OS?

A lightweight app that makes four things explicit so your team doesn't fall
apart quietly:

1. **Who decides what?** — clear ownership, not guessing
2. **Who does the work vs. who's just informed?** — so no one silently
   carries the team or coasts
3. **What did we actually agree to?** — decisions on the record, not
   someone's memory
4. **Is the team actually healthy?** — anonymous, honest, checked regularly

It's not a task tracker and it's not Jira. It's the structure layer that
most teams skip and then regret.

---

## What you need to do

### At setup (once, ~5 minutes)

Your team lead or owner will set up the app. You'll be asked to:

1. **Confirm you're on the Charter page** — check that your name and role
   are listed. Read the charter (mission, values, how-we-decide, credit
   rules). If you agree, acknowledge it in your team channel.

2. **Check the RACI page** — find your name in the grid. For each
   workstream, you should see your assignment:
   - **A** = you're Accountable (you own the outcome)
   - **R** = you're Responsible (you do the work)
   - **C** = you're Consulted (someone asks your input before deciding)
   - **I** = you're Informed (you're told after it's done)
   - Blank = you're not involved in that area

   If your assignments don't look right, raise it with the team. Better
   now than in a "who was supposed to do this?" argument later.

### Every week (~5 minutes)

- **Join the Level 10 meeting** — a structured weekly sync. The agenda is
  always the same:
  - Share a win (personal or work)
  - Scorecard: are the numbers on/off track?
  - Rocks: are your priorities on/off track?
  - Headlines: quick updates
  - IDS: the team works through the Issues List together
  - Conclude: recap what was decided

- **If you own a Scorecard metric** — enter this week's number on the
  Scorecard page before the meeting. It takes 30 seconds.

- **If you own a Rock** — update the status (on track / off track / done)
  on the Rocks page. "Off track" isn't a failure — it's a flag that the
  team should help unblock it at the Level 10.

### When something's wrong

- **Raise an issue on the Level 10 page.** Type what the problem is and
  who raised it. It goes on the Issues List and gets worked through at the
  next meeting using IDS (Identify the real problem → Discuss briefly →
  Solve with a decision and an owner).

- **Don't sit on it.** The Issues List exists so problems get surfaced
  early, not bottled up. If you wait until the deadline, it's too late
  for the team to help.

### When the Health Pulse comes around (monthly or biweekly)

You'll get a link to the Health Pulse page. Answer 8 questions on a 1–5
scale. Takes about 2 minutes.

**Read this part carefully — it's about your protection:**

- **Your answers are anonymous.** There is no name, email, or identifier
  attached to your response. This isn't a policy — it's how the data is
  built. There is literally no field to store who you are.

- **Results are hidden until enough people respond.** The app requires a
  minimum number of responses before showing *any* results. This means
  your individual answers can't be isolated by elimination ("only three
  people responded, and two of them said 5, so the 2 must be…").

- **Be honest.** The whole point of structural anonymity is so you can be.
  If your team lead isn't open to feedback, the pulse will detect it —
  there's a specific question about whether feedback actually leads to
  change. If that score stays low over time, it names the real problem.

## What protects you

Team OS is designed with a specific principle: **protect team members from
unhealthy leadership dynamics.** Here's how:

### The Decision Log is append-only

No one can edit or delete a past decision. If a decision is reversed, a
*new* entry is created that supersedes the old one — but the original stays
on the record. This means:

- "We never agreed to that" arguments have an answer
- Quiet revisionism (changing the story of what was decided) is visible
- You can always point to the log

### The Health Pulse is structurally anonymous

Not "we promise we won't look" anonymous. There is no author field in the
data at all. Even someone with access to the raw JSON file cannot tell
who submitted which response.

### The RACI makes ownership visible

When everyone can see who's Accountable for what, it's harder for one person
to silently hoard control, harder for someone to coast without it showing,
and harder for credit to be taken by the wrong person.

### The Coach names uncomfortable truths

If leadership openness scores very low, the app will say so directly — it
won't paper over it with more process. This matters because most tools
assume good-faith leadership and break silently when it's absent.

| Raise a problem | Level 10 page → add to Issues List |
| Check a past decision | Decision Log page — newest first |
| Update my Rock status | Rocks page — change the dropdown |
| Enter my Scorecard number | Scorecard page — fill in this week's column |

| See what the team should improve | Health Pulse page → "Results + Coach" tab |
| Get a summary for my channel | Export Hub page → Chat tab |

## What Team OS is *not*

- **Not a task tracker.** It doesn't replace Trello, Asana, or your to-do
  list. It tracks *ownership and decisions*, not individual tasks.
- **Not a communication tool.** It doesn't replace Slack or Discord. Use
  the Export Hub to push summaries *to* your chat tool.
- **Not a performance review.** The pulse measures *team* health, not
  individual performance. No one is scored or ranked.
- **Not optional once adopted.** If your team agrees to use it, the
  charter and RACI are your shared contract. "I didn't check the app"
  isn't a defense for missing an assignment you agreed to own.

---

## If something feels off

- If your RACI assignments don't match reality (you're doing work you're
  not assigned, or assigned work you can't do), raise it as an issue at the
  Level 10. The RACI should reflect how the team *actually* works, not how
  someone wishes it worked.

- If the same issue keeps coming back unresolved, that's a signal the IDS
  process isn't working — often because the real root cause is being
  avoided. Name it directly.

- If your pulse feedback never leads to change, the app's Coach will
  eventually flag it as a leadership problem. You don't have to be the one
  to say it — the anonymous aggregate will say it for you. That's the
  design.

- If none of the above helps, the honest answer is that no tool can fix a
  team where the leader is unwilling to listen. The decision log and pulse
  history are still yours — they're a truthful record of what happened,
  which matters whether or not the team improves.
